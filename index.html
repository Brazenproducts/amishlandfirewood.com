Small Farm and Amish Firewood: What to Know

Small farm and Amish firewood operations represent the traditional tier of the firewood market. These operations typically harvest hardwood from their own land or nearby sources, split it to consistent sizes, and air-dry for at least one season before sale. Quality varies significantly by operation, but the best small-farm firewood offers excellent species quality and honest moisture representation.

What to ask: species (oak? hickory? or mixed?), when it was split (this year or last year?), moisture content, and whether they have a moisture meter you can check. Reputable small operations welcome these questions and provide honest answers.

Big Box Store Firewood: The Honest Assessment

Home Depot and Lowe's typically sell firewood in 0.75 cubic foot bundles for $6 to $9. This equates to $300 to $500 per face cord equivalent in per-volume cost, far above local firewood pricing. Quality is inconsistent: moisture content is rarely specified, species is often unlabeled, and you frequently receive a mix of softwood and low-BTU hardwood.

Big box store firewood is convenient for occasional campfire use when you need a small amount quickly. For heating or any regular use, it is expensive and unreliable. The lack of moisture verification is the biggest issue: many bundles sold as seasoned contain wood at 30 to 40 percent moisture.

Buying Firewood Online: Amazon and Specialty Retailers

Amazon kiln-dried firewood bundles offer the best combination of quality certification and convenience for buyers who need reliable performance. Products from verified brands like Cutting Edge Firewood and Better Wood Products specify moisture content and species, allowing informed purchasing decisions. Delivery to your door eliminates hauling.

The trade-off is price: Amazon firewood costs more per BTU than bulk local purchasing. For regular heating use, local sourcing is more economical. Amazon excels for occasional campfire use, gifting, and buyers who want verified quality without the risk of local source variability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about buy firewood online, updated based on current research and reader feedback.

Where is the best place to buy firewood online?

Amazon offers the best selection of certified kiln-dried firewood with verified moisture content and clear species labeling. Look for brands like Cutting Edge Firewood, Better Wood Products, and Billy Buckskin for consistent quality. Read reviews specifically mentioning moisture content and dryness.

How much does firewood cost per cord?

Prices vary significantly by region and quality. Air-dried hardwood runs $150 to $300 per face cord or $450 to $900 per full cord in most regions. Kiln-dried adds a 30 to 50 percent premium. Big box store bundles cost $300 to $600 per face cord equivalent. Local small farms typically offer the best price for quality hardwood.

What should I ask when buying firewood from a local supplier?

Key questions: What species is it? When was it split? What is the moisture content? Can I check with a moisture meter? Is it sold by true cord or some informal measure? How much does a full cord weigh? Reputable suppliers answer these questions confidently.

Is it better to buy firewood in spring or fall?

Spring purchasing gives you the full summer to stack and air-dry freshly split wood, allowing adequate seasoning before winter. Fall purchasing typically means buying already-seasoned wood from the previous year at slightly higher demand pricing. Spring is better if you plan ahead; fall is necessary if you did not.

Can I buy firewood by the face cord or should I always buy a full cord?

Face cords (one row approximately 4x8 feet, logs 16 inches deep) are practical for many buyers. A full cord is three times that volume. Buy the quantity you expect to use in one season to avoid year-over-year storage degradation. Most buyers who heat primarily with wood need 3 to 5 full cords.

How do I spot low-quality firewood before buying?

Check the weight: underweight wood may be softwood mislabeled as hardwood. Look for moisture: fresh-cut end grain is visibly wet. Smell: green wood smells like fresh-cut lumber. Ask for moisture meter verification. Inspect species: bark characteristics identify most common species.

What is the difference between a cord, face cord, and rick of firewood?

A full cord is a standard measurement: 4 feet high by 4 feet wide by 8 feet long of stacked wood, containing 128 cubic feet. A face cord (or rick) is typically 4x8 feet stacked with 16-inch logs, which is approximately one-third of a full cord. Always clarify the measurement before purchasing.

Is Amazon a good source for regular firewood needs?

Amazon is excellent for occasional use, campfire wood, and quality verification. For primary heating needs requiring 3 to 5 cords per season, local bulk purchasing is significantly more economical. Many buyers use Amazon for their starting kindling and fatwood while sourcing their bulk heating wood locally.
